Waakye

Quick & Authentic Ghanaian Waakye

A beloved Ghanaian street food classic, Waakye is a simple dish of rice and beans cooked with special leaves for a distinct colour and flavour. Serve it with a variety of sides for the full experience.

Serves: 4-6

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Black-Eyed Peas, soaked overnight
  • 2 cups Long-Grain White Rice, washed
  • 8-10 Dried Waakye (Sorghum) Leaves
    • (Alternative: 1/4 tsp baking soda for colour, not flavour)
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • Water

Classic Sides: Tomato Stew, Shito (hot sauce), Gari Foto, Boiled Spaghetti, Hard-Boiled Egg, Fried Plantain, Sliced Avocado.

Instructions

  1. Cook the Beans: Place the soaked beans and Waakye leaves in a large pot. Cover with water and boil for 20-30 minutes until the beans are partially tender. The water will turn a deep red.
  2. Add the Rice: Remove and discard the Waakye leaves. Add the washed rice and salt to the pot. Add just enough hot water to cover the mixture by about 1.5 cm (1/2 inch).
  3. Steam: Bring to a quick boil, then immediately reduce heat to the absolute lowest setting. Cover the pot with a tight lid and let it steam for 20-25 minutes. It is crucial that you do not stir it.
  4. Rest & Serve: Turn off the heat and let the Waakye rest, still covered, for 10 minutes. Fluff gently with a fork and serve hot with your favourite sides.
