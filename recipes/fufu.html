Fufu

How to Make Authentic Ghanaian Fufu

Fufu is a soft, stretchy, dough-like swallow, fundamental to Ghanaian cuisine. It is not eaten by itself, but serves as the perfect, hearty accompaniment to rich and flavourful soups. The authentic version is made by pounding boiled cassava and green plantain, a rhythmic process that creates a uniquely smooth and tender result. For convenience, a powdered version is also widely used.

Method 1: Traditional Pounded Fufu (The Authentic Way)

This method requires strength and rhythm, and is often a two-person job. The result is unparalleled in texture and taste.

Yields: 3-4 servings
Prep time: 15 minutes
Cook time: 45-60 minutes

Ingredients & Equipment:

  • 1 medium-sized Cassava Root
  • 2 semi-ripe Green Plantains
  • Water for boiling
  • A pinch of salt (optional)

Essential Equipment:

  • A large, heavy Mortar (called Waduro)
  • A heavy wooden Pestle (called Woma)

Instructions:

  1. Peel and Prepare: Using a sharp, sturdy knife, peel the thick brown skin off the cassava and the green skin off the plantains.
  2. Chop: Cut the cassava and plantain into small, roughly 2-inch chunks. Be sure to remove the tough, stringy core from the center of the cassava.
  3. Boil: Place the chunks in a large pot. Add the pinch of salt and cover generously with water. Bring to a boil and cook for about 20-25 minutes, or until the cassava and plantain are very soft and can be easily pierced with a fork.
  4. Prepare for Pounding: Drain all the hot water from the pot, but you may reserve a small bowl of it for later.
  5. Pound the Fufu:
    • Place the boiled plantain into the mortar first. The plantain is softer and creates a good base. Begin pounding it into a smooth, sticky paste.
    • Once the plantain is smooth, add the cassava chunks in batches.
    • Pound with firm, rhythmic strokes. Traditionally, a second person sits by the mortar, turning the dough with their hand between strikes, using the reserved hot water to keep their fingers from sticking.
    • Safety Note: If you are pounding alone, pound at a slower pace. Stop periodically to use the pestle or your wetted hand to fold and turn the dough.
    • Continue pounding until all lumps are gone and you have a smooth, soft, and elastic dough.
  6. Shape and Serve: Wet your hands and shape the fufu into smooth, round portions. Place in a serving bowl and serve immediately with hot soup.
