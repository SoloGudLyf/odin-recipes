Classic Ghana Jollof Rice

This recipe guides you through creating a vibrant and deeply flavourful Jollof Rice, a beloved staple across West Africa. The key is to build a rich, aromatic tomato and pepper base before slowly steaming the rice to perfection.

Serves: 4 – 6
Prep time: 15 minutes
Cook time: 50 – 60 minutes

Ingredients

For the Sauce Base:

  • 6 ripe, large tomatoes, quartered
  • 2 large onions, divided
  • 1-2 fresh red chillies (like Scotch Bonnet or Habanero), optional
  • 6 cloves of garlic
  • 2 tablespoons of double-concentrate tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup (approx. 60ml) vegetable oil or other neutral cooking oil

For the Rice and Protein:

  • 500g of your choice of protein (beef, chicken, or lamb), cut into bite-sized pieces
    • Vegetarian Alternative: 500g of firm vegetables like carrots, bell peppers, and green beans.
  • 800g (approx. 4 cups) long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1.5 litres (approx. 6 cups) of quality beef, chicken, or vegetable stock. Alternatively, use water and 1-2 stock cubes.
  • 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme (optional, but recommended)
  • 1 bay leaf (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Preparation

  1. Create the Pepper Purée:
    In a blender, combine the 6 tomatoes, one of the large onions (roughly chopped), the garlic cloves, and the chillies (if using). Blend until you have a smooth, consistent purée. Set this aside.
  2. Sear the Protein:
    Heat the vegetable oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. If using raw meat or chicken, season it lightly with salt and pepper. Add the protein to the hot oil and sear on all sides until nicely browned. Remove the protein with a slotted spoon and set it aside, leaving the flavourful oil in the pot.
  3. Build the Flavour Base: Dice the second large onion. Reduce the heat to medium, add the diced onion to the pot and sauté for 5-7 minutes until softened and translucent. Add the tomato paste and cook for another 3-4 minutes, stirring constantly. This step caramelises the paste, deepening the flavour and giving the Jollof its signature rich red colour.
  4. Simmer the Sauce: Pour the blended tomato purée into the pot. Add your stock (or water and stock cubes), the ground white and black pepper, dried thyme, and the bay leaf. Stir everything together well. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to a steady simmer. Cover and cook for about 10-15 minutes to allow the flavours to meld together.
  5. Season Generously: Uncover the sauce and taste it. At this stage, it needs to be seasoned boldly with salt. The rice will absorb a significant amount of salt and flavour, so the sauce should taste slightly more seasoned than you think is necessary.
  6. Cook the Rice: Rinse your long-grain parboiled rice under cool water until the water runs clear. Add the rinsed rice to the simmering sauce and stir thoroughly to ensure every grain is coated.
  7. Steam to Perfection: Reduce the heat to the absolute lowest setting. Cover the pot tightly with a lid. For an even better seal, you can place a layer of aluminium foil over the pot before putting the lid on. Let it steam, undisturbed, for 20-25 minutes.
  8. The Final Mix: Uncover the pot. The rice should have absorbed most of the liquid. Add your seared protein (or mixed vegetables) back into the pot. Gently fluff and fold the rice with a fork to combine. If the rice seems too dry, you can add up to 1 cup of hot water or stock.
  9. Finish Cooking: Replace the foil and lid and allow it to steam for a final 10-15 minutes, or until the rice is tender and fluffy.
  10. Rest and Serve: Turn off the heat and let the Jollof rice stand, covered, for at least 5-10 minutes. This lets the grains settle and absorbs the last bit of steam. Before serving, fluff one last time with a fork. It is delicious served on its own or with a side of fried plantain and a simple salad.
